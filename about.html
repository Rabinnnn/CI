COME INITIATIVE

Community Mentorship and Empowerment (COME) Initiative is a Youth Led-Community Based Organization that Creates a Supportive Environment FOR Youth, Adolescent Girls and Young Women to Build Sustainable, Peaceful, Tolerant and Inclusive Community through Life Skills, Talent and ICT Development.

Current Ongoing Projects

Currently on Cause

SIKIKA project builds the skills of content generators in Kakuma Refugee Camp to produce quality and compelling audio content that informs the public, objectively and accurately on the issues that shape their lives by allowing them to debate and facilitate dialogue across marginalized population and addr-essing peoples con-cerns in their local dialect.

Currently on Cause

JIAJIRI aims to provide high quality vocational training skills, life skills and work readiness skills for the youth through capacity building on ICT, Entrepreneurship and Dressmaking for socio-economic development.

Currently on Cause

JIJUE Creates a safe place for high Quality Life Skills Based and increased open discuss-ion on Reproductive Health, Mental health, Sexuality and Gender issues among Adolesce-nt Girls and Young Women, including PWD through multistakehold-ers’ involvement.

Currently on Cause

VIVA seeks to empower youth to improve their own sexual, reproduct-ive health and socio-economic situations. The primary outcome of ViVA project is improved sexual and reproductive health outcomes and socioeconomic wellbe-ing of Kenyan youth aged 15-24 years residing in informal settlements in Kisumu.

Media Development

COME's approach to media development includes tailored activities that are context-sensitive, and which also take account of the challenges and opportunities created by the rapidly changing media environment. We use the power of media and communication to help reduce poverty, improve health, save lives and support people in understanding their rights. Our aim is to inform, connect and empower people around the world. We harness our creativity on TV, radio, online and social media. We support other media organisations to create content too. We conduct research to inform, monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of our media projects.
